About Me

Hi! I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Swinburne University of Technology, Melbourne. I have done my PhD in Internet of Things under the joint supervision of Prof. Prem Prakash Jayaraman and Prof. Dimitrios Georgakopoulos from Swinburne University of Technology, Melbourne in 2023. My area of interest includes IoT, Edge and Fog Computing, Distributed Systems and Computer Networks. My PhD dissertation is on evaluating quality in IoT. Previous to my doctoral studies, I have done Masters by Research from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Ropar, India in 2019. My masters thesis is on proposing scheduling algorithms for heterogeneous fog networks. During my masters, I have also been a visiting research scholar at Ontario Tech University, Canada. I have more than six years of research experience and have published high impact peer reviewed journals and conferences (including core A*/A) publications.

Research Interest and Projects

Throughout my academic journey, I've been fascinated by Internet of Things, Fog/Edge Computing, Distributed Systems, Sensor Networks, Applied Machine Learning. Specifically, my research interests are as follows.
- Evaluating Quality of Autonomic IoT Applications.
- Scheduling Algorithms for Cloud, Edge/Fog Heterogeneous Systems.
- Modelling and Developing Distributed Systems.
- Data Analysis and Visualization.

Skills and Experiences

With a strong background in distributed computing and data science, I've brushed up my skills in various programming languages including Python, C/C++, SQL, Ruby, and Java, and have substantial experience with data analysis tools and libraries such as Visual Studio, Code Blocks, Eclipse/NetBeans, PhpStorm, Pycharm, Sci-kit Learn, and TensorFlow. My ability to handle large datasets, perform intricate analysis, and develop efficient software solutions has been essential to my research and professional development.
